How it works

  1. Point camera at the body part (knee, shoulder, wrist)
  2. Place your real hand on the body part — cover with a thin napkin
  3. Select record length, tap Record — do one massage stroke
  4. Virtual posterized hand loops over the live camera view
  5. Phone vibrates in sync — brain remaps ownership to the virtual hand
  6. Tap Hand Removed — loop continues, sensation may persist

Clinical context

Body ownership remapping via synchronized visual-tactile stimulation has shown efficacy in phantom limb pain, CRPS, and post-surgical rehabilitation. Sensation persistence after hand removal indicates successful proprioceptive drift.

Botvinick & Cohen (1998) · Moseley et al. (2008) · Ehrsson (2012)
